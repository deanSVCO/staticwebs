Conway's Game of Life - the introduction

Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton. Consider an MxN array of living cells. A cell can neither live alone nor in too large a crowd. Cells in healthy neighborhoods are born. With each generation (iteration), the health of the neighborhood is calculated and cells survive, perish or are born accordingly.

Conway's rules are thus:
  • Live cells with less than two neighbors die.
  • Live cells with more than three neighbors die.
  • Live cells with two or three neighbors survive.
  • Dead cells with three neighbors are born.
These simple rules, when applied to an initial state, have surprisingly complex results. Wikipedia has a good introduction, including examples of stable constructs, oscillating constructs and oscillating and moving constructs. Try building these constructs in the demo and seeing for yourself.
